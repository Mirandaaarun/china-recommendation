Travel Brief

China Trip Final Recommendation

Four cities. Zero wasted days. Built for a fast-paced China trip focused on nightlife, culture, food, and visual spectacle without wasting time on soft detours.

Dates: May 14 to May 24, 2026 Logged: April 7, 2026

The Route

Shanghai → Chongqing → Xi'an → Beijing

Nightlife Culture Food Visual Spectacle No Dead Time
This route opens with the easiest landing, peaks with the most visually intense city, anchors the middle with ancient history, and closes with the Great Wall.

Skipped

  • Chengdu: Comfortable, but Chongqing hits harder for this itinerary.
  • Shenzhen: Humid in May and not worth the detour.
  • Hangzhou: Beautiful, but too quiet for a 20s group.

Why These Four

Shanghai

Best U.S. flight connections, easiest English environment, and the strongest soft landing into China. It gives the trip immediate polish without culture shock.

Chongqing

The key call. Chengdu is comfortable; Chongqing is unforgettable. The skyline is pure cyberpunk, the river cruise is one of the sharpest visuals in Asia, and the nightlife has real depth.

Xi'an

Non-negotiable. The Terracotta Army is singular, and the city delivers one of the best old-China to night-market contrasts anywhere in the country.

Beijing

The strongest ending. The Great Wall lands harder as the last major set piece, and the city still closes with serious food and underground nightlife.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day City Plan
May 14 Arrive Shanghai Settle in. Shouning Road Night Market for dinner. Yongkang Lu for drinks.
May 15 Shanghai Anfu Road in the morning, The Bund at dusk, then Found 158 for Arkham or TAXX.
May 16 Shanghai Wanda Diamond League at Shanghai Stadium, then West Bund Museum and the Pompidou exhibition.
May 17 Fly to Chongqing Arrive in the afternoon. See Hongya Cave from the opposite bank at night, then go to an air raid shelter bar.
May 18 Chongqing Liziba Station early, Chaotianmen and the Crown Escalator, night river cruise, 9th Street clubs, then midnight hotpot.
May 19 Chongqing → Xi'an 8 a.m. high-speed rail, arrival around 1:30 p.m., then Sajinqiao Night Market and bowl-smashing liquor at Yongxingfang.
May 20 Xi'an Terracotta Army at 8:30 a.m., Shaanxi History Museum, then the Muslim Quarter at night.
May 21 Xi'an → Beijing Morning bike ride on the city wall, flight to Beijing, then Ghost Street at night.
May 22 Beijing Forbidden City early, Jingshan Park for the palace view, then Sanlitun in the evening.
May 23 Beijing Mutianyu Great Wall with a 7 a.m. start, then Dada Beijing at night.
May 24 Depart Beijing Head to PEK airport.

Three Best Nights

  1. Shanghai, May 15: French Concession dinner, then Arkham. Strongest club night of the trip.
  2. Chongqing, May 18: River cruise, 9th Street until 3 a.m., then midnight hotpot. Hard to beat.
  3. Beijing, May 23: Great Wall by day, Dada Beijing by night. Perfect contrast.

Three Best Days Overall

  1. May 17, Chongqing arrival night: The first full view of Hongya Cave from across the Jialing River is the kind of moment that resets your expectations.
  2. May 23, Great Wall: Mutianyu in May, fully green. This is one of the main reasons people come to China.
  3. May 20, Xi'an: Terracotta Army in the morning and the Muslim Quarter at night. Best one-day range of the whole trip.

City Quick Reference

Shanghai

  • Stay: French Concession or Jing'an.
  • Don't miss: Anfu Road, The Bund at dusk, Found 158, Speak Low, West Bund Museum.
  • Eat: Yang's Fried Dumplings, Shouning Road Night Market, dinner in the French Concession.

Chongqing

  • Stay: Jiefangbei or Jiangbei near 9th Street.
  • Don't miss: Hongya Cave from across the river, Liziba Station before 9 a.m., the night cruise, 9th Street, an air raid shelter bar, midnight hotpot.
  • Eat: Xiaomian, Zhoushixiong Hotpot, hot and sour noodles.
  • Skip: Inside Hongya Cave and Nanbin Road bars.

Xi'an

  • Stay: Inside or near the city wall, close to the Muslim Quarter.
  • Don't miss: Terracotta Army, Shaanxi History Museum, Muslim Quarter, Yongxingfang, Sajinqiao Night Market.
  • Eat: Roujiamo, biangbiang noodles, yangrou paomo, liangpi.

Beijing

  • Stay: Sanlitun or Gulou.
  • Don't miss: Forbidden City, Jingshan Park, Mutianyu Great Wall, Dada Beijing, Ghost Street.
  • Eat: Da Dong Peking Duck, breakfast jianbing, midnight lamb-spine hotpot on Ghost Street.
  • Skip: Wangfujing Snack Street.
